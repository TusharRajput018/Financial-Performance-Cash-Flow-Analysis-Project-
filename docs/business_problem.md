

##  Business Problem

Many organizations track revenue and expenses at a high level but lack visibility into **where profits fluctuate, which departments drive costs, and how payment delays impact cash flow**.

In this case, we are working with transaction-level financial data that includes revenue, expenses, departments, categories, due dates, and settlement dates. While the company appears profitable overall, management does not have clear answers to the following questions:

* How does profitability change month over month?
* Which departments or categories are contributing most to expenses?
* Is profit stable, or is it being pressured by rising costs?
* Are customers paying on time?
* How much cash is getting delayed due to late settlements?
* Which counterparties are responsible for the highest delayed amounts?

The absence of structured analysis makes it difficult for decision-makers to:

* Control operational costs
* Improve liquidity
* Detect financial risk early
* Make informed budgeting decisions

---

##  Objective of This Project

The primary objective of this project is to transform raw transaction data into a structured analytical view that provides clarity on:

1. Overall financial performance (Revenue, Expenses, Profit)
2. Department-level profitability
3. Category-level cost behavior
4. Monthly performance trends
5. Payment delays and their impact on cash flow

By building a clean data model and interactive dashboard, the goal is to support management in:

* Monitoring financial health consistently
* Identifying cost-heavy areas
* Understanding delay patterns in receivables
* Reducing financial risk through better visibility

---

##  Why This Matters

Profitability alone does not guarantee financial stability.
If significant revenue is tied up in delayed payments, the business may experience liquidity stress even while reporting strong profits.

This project bridges the gap between:

* Accounting-level reporting
* And decision-oriented financial intelligence

It shifts the focus from just "How much did we earn?" to:

* "Where are we spending?"
* "Are we getting paid on time?"
* "What operational areas require attention?"

---

##  Expected Outcome

After implementing this analysis, stakeholders should be able to:

* Track performance trends over time
* Detect unusual cost spikes
* Identify departments affecting margins
* Quantify delayed cash exposure
* Prioritize follow-ups on high-risk invoices

This creates a foundation for smarter financial planning and improved operational control.
