## Executive Summary

This project was undertaken to better understand the company’s financial performance and identify areas where operational behavior may be affecting profitability and cash flow. While revenue and expense data were available at the transaction level, there was no structured way to evaluate trends, cost drivers, or payment delays.

The analysis focused on transforming raw transaction records into a clear view of how the business is performing over time and where attention is needed.

---

##  Key Focus Areas

The work addressed three main questions:

1. How is the business performing financially month over month?
2. Which departments and categories are driving expenses?
3. Are payments being received on time, or is cash getting delayed?

By combining SQL-based aggregation with Power BI modeling, the project moved from basic data storage to actionable insight.

---

##  What the Analysis Found

* The business is profitable overall, but profitability varies across months rather than remaining constant.
* A limited number of departments and categories account for a significant share of total expenses, suggesting opportunities for tighter cost monitoring.
* Payment delays are present and create periods where revenue is recognized but cash is not yet received.
* Certain counterparties contribute more heavily to delayed settlements, indicating potential collection or follow-up improvements.
* Transaction volume helps explain fluctuations, showing whether changes in performance are due to scale or cost pressure.

---

##  Dashboard Outcome

A three-page Power BI dashboard was developed to support decision-making:

* **Executive Overview** provides a snapshot of revenue, expenses, profit, and transaction trends.
* **Cost Analysis** highlights where money is being spent and which operational areas influence margins.
* **Cash Flow & Risk** focuses on delayed payments and their impact on liquidity.

This structure allows both management and operational teams to quickly understand performance without reviewing raw financial records.

---

##  Business Value

The project demonstrates how transaction-level data can be used not just for reporting, but for monitoring financial behavior.
It enables stakeholders to:

* Track performance trends consistently
* Identify cost-heavy operational areas
* Detect payment delays early
* Improve visibility into how financial outcomes are formed

Rather than replacing accounting reports, this dashboard complements them by providing a practical analytical layer for ongoing decision support.
